My Projects

Currently most of my prjects have taken place during my Study of Creative Technology at the University of Twente. Over the past 5 modules I have partaken in multiple hackathons, as well as finished a plethora of assignments, which yielded magnificent results.

The most notable one from module 2 is a project conducted in the range of an assignment, in which we were promted to recreate a invention by Leonardo Da Vinci. I chose to recreate the design of an aerial screw via blender and implemented basic user interaction using unity and WebGL.
In module 4 the project focused more on the creative part of the curriculum. It follows that we designed a interactive art installation for the chosen technology/topic of origami. Our idea was to create a automatic mechanism which folded and unfolded a origami piece, which contained a japanese haiku talking about one of the many meanings of origami.
Module 5 was our elective module, in which i chose the track Smart Tech. Smart Tech focused more on the electrical engineering side of things, such as PID controllers or interfacing analog sensors. This theory accumulated in the final project in which we were presented the task to build a robot which could solve a mace. More requirements were that the goal of the robot was given to him with a supersonic frequency, which it needed to demodulate. To recognize the final position we added a barcode scaner to the robot which stopped when it red the correct bar code. The movement was hard coded, as that yielded the fastest result. Based on this result we won the first place in the accompanying competion.
